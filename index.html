Koukl's Tactics

"Put a stone in their shoe." — Gregg Koukl

THE COLUMBO TACTIC
1
"What do you mean by that?"
Clarify before you counter. You can't answer a vague claim.
2
"How did you come to that conclusion?"
Shift the burden of proof back to the claimant.
3
"Have you considered…?"
Gently introduce a fact, tension, or challenge.
🔄 Take the Roof Off

Accept their premise temporarily and follow the logic to an absurd conclusion. Lewis + Koukl combo. "If morality is just social conditioning, why should I follow your morality?"

💥 Suicide Tactics

Statements that refute themselves: "There is no absolute truth" — is that absolutely true? "You can't know anything for certain" — are you certain?

⚖️ Sibling Rivalry Tactic

Show that the objection applies equally to competing worldviews. "You said Christians have done evil — but atheist regimes killed more people in 100 years than religion did in all history."

🪞 Rhodes Tactic

Ask them to defend their own worldview. "I'm curious — what do YOU think happens when we die?" Now you're listening, not defending.
